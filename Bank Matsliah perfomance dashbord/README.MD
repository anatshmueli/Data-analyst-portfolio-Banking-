# 🏦 Banking Data Analysis Dashboard | Power BI

## 📖 Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end **Business Intelligence (BI) solution** developed in **Power BI** to analyze banking operations and customer activity between **2018 and 2023**.

The project transforms raw banking data into meaningful business insights through interactive dashboards, KPIs, data modeling, and advanced DAX calculations.

The analysis focuses on customer account activity, branch performance, deposit trends, customer complaints, and account closures, enabling decision-makers to monitor performance, identify trends, and support strategic business decisions.

---

# 🎯 Project Objectives

The primary objective of this project is to demonstrate how Business Intelligence tools can be used to transform banking data into actionable insights.

The dashboard was designed to:

- Analyze customer account activity over time.
- Compare Savings and Current account performance.
- Identify the highest-performing bank branches.
- Analyze deposit trends across different years and quarters.
- Monitor customer complaints and account closures.
- Explore the relationship between customer complaints and customer churn.
- Build interactive reports that support business decision-making.

---

# 🗄 Data Model

The Power BI model is based on a relational banking database consisting of multiple connected tables.

Main tables include:

- 👨‍💼 Bankers
- 👥 Clients
- 💳 Accounts
- 💰 Deposits
- 🏦 Loans
- 💳 Cards
- 📅 Date Dimension
- 🏢 Branch Dimension

Relationships between the tables were created to support efficient filtering, aggregation, and business analysis.

# 📈 Key Business Insights

The analysis revealed several important business insights:

- 2019 recorded the highest number of newly opened bank accounts.
- Branches 12 and 92 achieved the strongest performance.
- Deposit activity reached its highest level during Q4 2022.
- Account closures increased significantly during the final quarter of 2023.
- Customer complaints showed a moderate positive correlation with account closures, suggesting that customer satisfaction may influence customer retention.
